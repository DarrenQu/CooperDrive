Method

CooperDrive combines multi-task BEV perception, semantic localization, cooperative object sharing, and downstream path planning in a unified framework for real-world deployable cooperative driving.

Highlights

Multi-task BEV perception

A shared BEV backbone jointly supports 3D object detection and semantic localization, improving efficiency and consistency.

Semantic localization

Earlier BEV features are reused to estimate accurate vehicle poses in a shared world frame, enabling reliable multi-agent alignment.

Lightweight sharing

CooperDrive exchanges compact object-level results instead of raw sensor data or heavy BEV features, making communication practical for real vehicles.

Planning-aware benefit

Cooperative perception directly improves downstream planning by revealing occluded hazards earlier, without changing the existing planner architecture.

Across all tested scenarios, cooperative perception improves safety margin and enables earlier, less aggressive planning maneuvers.

Metric definitions.

  • TTCmin: shortest predicted time to a potential collision. Higher is safer.
  • DRAC: minimum deceleration required to avoid a crash. Lower is safer and smoother.
  • DCZ: shortest distance to a predicted conflict zone. Higher indicates a larger safety margin.
  • VR: percentage of planning steps that violate safety constraints. Lower is better.
